Climate Change

Climate change refers to the changes in tempertures and weather conditions. These can be natural or man-made.
However since the industrial revolution in Europe, human acticity has been the main cause of climate change, it has accelerated the rate of change.

Causes

We have already mentioned that climate change is caused by both human activity and by nature itself. Now we will dive a little further into this.

Natutral

Volcanic eruptions - sulfur dioxie released can potentially cause global cooling. Carbon dioxide release can cause global warming, due to it being a greenhouse gas.
Milankovitch cycles - change the shape of Earth's revoltuions around the Sun, called eccentricity . Axial tilt of the Earth with respect to its orbital plane, called obliquity. Change in the orientation of the rotational axis, called precession.

Artificial

Burning fossil fuels - releases greenhouses gases which cause the greenhouse effect, trapping heat inside.
Livestock farming - cows and other domestic animals release huge amounts of methane after eating, which also contributes to the greenhouse effect
